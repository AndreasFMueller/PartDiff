On the domain 
$
\Omega
=
\{ (x,y)\in\mathbb{R}^2 \mid y>0\}
$,
the linear partial differential operator
\[
L
=
\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2}
+
(2\!\sqrt{y}-y)
\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x\,\partial y}
-
2y^{\frac32}
\frac{\partial^2}{\partial y^2}
\]
of second order is given.
The direction of the $y$-axis is considered to be the direction
towards the future.
\begin{teilaufgaben}
\item
Is the operator $L$ elliptic, parabolic or hyperbolic?
\item
Which boundary values can influence the value of a solution
in the point $P=(1,1)$?
\end{teilaufgaben}

\begin{hinweis}
The factorization $2\!\sqrt{y}\cdot y=2y^{\frac32}$ may be useful
for the solution.
\end{hinweis}

\begin{loesung}
\begin{figure}[ht]
\centering
\includeagraphics[]{graph.pdf}
\caption{Characteristics in problem~\ref{90000035}
\label{90000035:fig}}
\end{figure}
\begin{teilaufgaben}
\item
The symbol matrix of the operator is
\[
A
=
\begin{pmatrix}
     1                        & \!\sqrt{y}-\frac12y \\
\!\sqrt{y\mathstrut}-\frac12y & -2y^{\frac32}
\end{pmatrix}
\]
with determinant
\[
\det A
=
-2y^{\frac32}
-(\!\sqrt{y}-{\textstyle\frac12}y)^2
<0.
\]
As the determinant is negative throughout the domain, the operator
is hyperbolic.
\item
The ordinary differential equation for the characteristics is
\[
\dot{y}^2
+
(y-2\!\sqrt{y})\dot{y}\dot{x}
-
2y^{\frac32}\dot{x}^2
=
0.
\]
Dividing by $\dot{x}$ and using $y'=\dot{y}/\dot{x}$ gives
\[
y^{\prime 2}
+(y-2\!\sqrt{y})y'
-2y^{\frac32}
=
0
\]
It has the factorization
\[
y^{\prime 2}
+(y-2\!\sqrt{y})y'
-2y^{\frac32}
=
(y'-2\!\sqrt{y})(y'+y)
=
0.
\]
This leads to the differential equations
\begin{align*}
y'-2\!\sqrt{y}&=0
&&\text{and}&
y'+y&=0
\intertext{with solutions}
\frac{y'}{2\!\sqrt{y}}&=1
&&&
\frac{y'}{y}&=-1
\\
\int\frac{dy}{2\!\sqrt{y}} &= \int dx
&&&
\int\frac{dy}{y} &= -\int dx
\\
\!\sqrt{y}&= x + C
&&&
\log y &= -x + D
\\
y&= (x+C)^2
&&&
y&= e^{-x+D}.
\end{align*}
So the characterstics are parabolas touching the $x$-axis and 
exponential curves, as shown in figure~\ref{90000035:fig}.
Note that the equation $y'=2\!\sqrt{y}$ implies that the slope must be
positive, so only the increasing part of the parabola is a characteristic
curve.

The characteristic curves through the point $P=(1,1)$ are
\begin{align*}
y&=x^2
&
y&=e^{1-x}.
\end{align*}
The first starts at $(0,0)$ on the $x$-axis, but the second does not
reach the $x$-axis ever, as the curve has the $x$-axis as an asymptote.
Thus all points on the positive $x$-axis can influence the value of
the solution in the point $P$.
\qedhere
\end{teilaufgaben}
\end{loesung}

\begin{bewertung}
Symbol matrix ({\bf A}) 1 point,
operator is hyperbolic ({\bf H}) 1 point,
differential equation of characteristics ({\bf D}) 1 point,
factorization or other method to convert them into two first order
differential equations ({\bf F}) 1 point,
characteristic curves ({\bf C}) 1 point,
domain of influence for the point $P$ ({\bf P}) 1 point.
\end{bewertung}
