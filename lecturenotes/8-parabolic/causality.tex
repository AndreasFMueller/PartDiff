\section{Causality}
The singular solution $K$ demonstrates that a change in $f$ at
$(t_0,x_0)$ affects all values of the solution $u(t,x)$ for $t>t_0$.
A disturbance at time $t_0$ propagates instantaneously throughout
the the $\Omega$.
